Sequence Matching Study

In this study, you will be asked to decide whether short sequences of states (“traces”) match an English temporal rule. Questions involve a panel of lights with three colors: Red, Green, and Blue. Use only the provided rule, not your prior intuition, to make your judgment.

How to Read a State

A state captures what is happening at one moment in time. It lists which lights are on and off at that moment. You can click on any trace to enlarge it.

"b & !g & !r"

How to Read a Trace

A trace is a sequence of states connected by arrows representing the passage of time. That is, each arrow means "the next moment in time". All traces in this study end in a cycle, shown with a loop/back-arrow. This means the final part of the trace repeats forever. For example:

Rule: "Every time the Blue light (b) is on, the Green light (g) must eventually turn on."
These traces match the rule
!b & !g & !r; b & !g & r; !b & g & !r; cycle{!b & !g & r}

The rule places no restrictions on the value of r. Its requirements concern only the relationship between b and g.

b & !g & !r; b & !g & !r; !b & g & !r; cycle{!b & !g & !r}

Even though the Blue light is on in two states (state 1 and state 2) and the Green light is only on in state 3, the rule has not been violated. The Green light turns on in a state after the states where the Blue light is on.

These traces do not match the rule.
b & !g & !r; cycle{!b & !g & !r}

The Blue light turns on in state 1, the Green light never turns on afterwards.

!b & g & !r; b & !g & !r; cycle{b & !g & !r}

The Green light is only on before the Blue light turns on (state 2); after the Blue light turns on, the Green light never turns on again.
